# The Courage to Change
### Practical and soulful tools for navigating uncertainty and transition with grace, resilience, and even joy.

*Working script v1 for a ~45-minute keynote. Warm, grounded, fireside — a wise companion
at the crossing, never a hype stage. Built for rooms in transition: career turns, life
pivots, organizations mid-change, anyone standing at an unknown. Read the **30-minute
cut** and **60-minute expansion** notes near the end before you set your timing.*

---

**Throughline:** The courage to change was never the blind leap we've been sold — it is moving forward into the unknown while grounded in who you are; and since being grounded in who you are requires feedback, the bravest act of any changing life turns out to be the quietest one: asking someone to tell you what they see.

**The one big idea:** Courage is never blind. What looks like the brave part of change — the leap, the announcement, the burning boat — is not where courage lives. Courage lives earlier and quieter: in being grounded in who you are, which requires feedback, which requires walking up to the one pane of your window you cannot see through and asking someone to open it from their side. **The Blind Spot is the portal to growth** — and the alternative to walking through it is not safety. It is stagnation and rot.

---

## The spine — three movements, through the window

The talk walks one idea through three movements. The window carries the whole visual arc: standing grounded before it (Grounded) → asking someone to open the blind pane (The Portal) → and stepping through into a larger self (Depth & Breadth).

**1. GROUNDED** *(Movement I — Sections 1, 2)* — what courage actually is.
> *Johnny, verbatim:* "Courage is never blind. Courage is moving forward into the unknown and being grounded in who you are."

Not fearlessness, not recklessness, not the cliff-jump highlight reel. Courage has its eyes open and its feet on the ground — and the ground is self-knowledge.

**2. THE PORTAL** *(Movement II — Sections 3, 4)* — feedback, the bravest act.
> *Johnny, verbatim:* "Being grounded in who you are requires feedback. And receiving feedback is perhaps the act that requires the most courage. The Blind Spot of Johari's window is the portal to growth. It takes us into vulnerable spaces. It requires us to trust the process of journeying forward."

The structural heart. The one pane of your life you cannot see through is the one everyone around you is looking through — and it only opens from the outside. Asking is the courage.

**3. DEPTH & BREADTH** *(Movement III — Sections 5, 6, 7)* — the self uncovered.
> *Johnny, verbatim:* "The alternative is stagnation and rot. In this way — courage leads us forward to our own health and allows us to uncover our unknown self and build upon our character, adding both depth and breadth."

Growth in two directions at once: deeper into who you already are, wider into who you haven't met yet. Health, not heroics.

**The arc in one line:** courage is never blind → it moves into the unknown grounded in who you are → grounding requires feedback → the blind pane is the portal, and it opens only from the outside → walking through is vulnerable and asks for trust in the journey → the alternative is stagnation and rot → through the portal the unknown self is uncovered, and character grows in depth and breadth.

## Section 1 — Cold open: the leap that wasn't the brave part
**Target: 5 min**

- Open on the myth, warmly: every story we're told about change stars a leap — the quit-the-job scene, the sold-the-house scene, the dramatic before-and-after. *"We've been sold a picture of courage with its eyes squeezed shut. - Name the counterfeit: blind leaps aren't courage — they're often just fear running in a new direction. *"I've watched people leap out of one performed life straight into another one. The scenery changed. The performance didn't."*

## Section 2 — Grounded: what courage actually stands on *(GROUNDED)*
**Target: 7 min** · *(Audience Moment 1 lands here)*

- Unwrap courage from its costumes, one at a time: courage isn't fearlessness (fear comes along, it just doesn't drive); it isn't certainty (the unknown stays unknown); it isn't speed (some of the bravest changes take years and look like nothing from the outside).
- Then the definition, again, slower — and the hinge inside it: *"Moving into the unknown, grounded in who you are. But hold on. Grounded in who you are — how do you actually know who you are?"* Let the question sit.
- The honest answer, Johnny's verbatim: *"**Being grounded in who you are requires feedback.**"* Unpack gently: the self-portrait we paint alone is always painted from the inside of the house. Some of who we are is only visible from the yard.
- Name the resistance with compassion: we say we want to grow, and we arrange our whole lives to avoid being told anything we don't already believe. Nods of agreement feel like friendship; mirrors feel like threats.
- Land the movement's reframe, Johnny's verbatim: *"**Receiving feedback is perhaps the act that requires the most courage.**"* — *"Not the leap. The listening."*
- **→ Audience Moment 1 (silent recognition):** *"No hands, just honesty: when is the last time you asked someone — actually asked — to tell you the truth about you? Not fishing for a compliment. The truth."* Hold ~15 seconds of real silence. Then, softly: *"Whatever came up — even if the answer was 'never' — that's not shame. That's just the map showing you where the courage is."*

*Speaker note: This section retires the action-hero definition of courage and installs Johnny's, verbatim, unsoftened. Never turn "requires feedback" into corporate 360-review language — this is soul-level, not HR-level. The silence after Moment 1 is load-bearing; don't rescue the room from it.*

## Section 3 — The window: the pane you cannot see through *(THE PORTAL)*
**Target: 10 min** *(the peak — protect this)*

- Introduce the window as a picture, not a framework — human first, never academic, but give the room the full map by name *(per Johnny, 2026-07-15: a clinical dive into Johari is for another day, but all four quadrants get a mention so the audience has the context)*: *"There's an old, simple picture of the self — psychologists call it the Johari Window, and it's just that: a window, with four panes. The **known self**, the **hidden self**, the **unknown self** — and the **blind spot**. That's as clinical as tonight gets. Now let me show you the window."* Build it physically, hands framing each pane:
  1. *"The first pane, everyone can see through — you and the world both. The **known self**: the you at the dinner table, the open pane."*
  2. *"The second pane, only YOU see through — the **hidden self**: what you keep behind the curtain. Your private rooms. Everyone has them."*
  3. *"The third pane, NOBODY sees through yet — the **unknown self**: the rooms even you haven't opened. Hold that one; we're coming back to it."*
  4. *"And the fourth pane… everyone can see through EXCEPT you. The **blind spot**."* **(pause)** *"Think about that. There is a whole pane of your life — your patterns, your effect on people, your gifts you discount, your edges you can't feel — in plain view of everyone who loves you. And you have never seen it once."*
- Johnny's story — the load-bearing wall: a real piece of feedback he received, hard to hear, that opened a pane he could not have opened alone. `[Johnny to fill: the feedback that changed you — who said it (named only as you're comfortable), what they saw that you couldn't, what it cost to hear, and what it opened. One true scene. If the truest version is "I avoided hearing it for years," tell THAT — the dismissal is the story.]`

### ★ THE PORTAL MOMENT — the single unforgettable beat
*Build it slowly, at fireside volume, hands still framing the blind pane. Real silence between steps.*

> *"Here's the thing about that fourth pane. I spent years treating it like a wall — something to defend, something to keep people away from."* **(pause)**

> *"It was never a wall. **The Blind Spot is the portal to growth.** It's the only pane with something behind it that you can't reach any other way. Every other pane, you can work alone. Not that one."* **(pause)**

> *"And here's what makes it courage: **that pane only opens from the outside.** You can't see through it — by definition. Someone else has to hand you what they see. Which means the doorway to your own growth… begins with a question you have to be brave enough to ask."* **(hold)**

- **THE REVEAL — drop the volume:** *"So the bravest thing you'll do this year probably won't look like a leap. It will look like sitting across from someone who knows you, and saying: 'What do you see that I don't?' — and then not defending yourself against the answer."*
- Hold two full seconds before moving on.

*Speaker note: This is the peak and the talk's feather-rock-truck equivalent — the four-pane build + the portal reveal, built in full exactly once. JOHARI RULE (Johnny, 2026-07-15): all four quadrants are NAMED once — known self · hidden self · unknown self · blind spot — so the room has the context, and that single naming is the entire clinical content of the talk; the deep dive is deliberately saved for another day. Attribution stays one clause (Joseph Luft and Harrington Ingham, 1955, if anyone asks after). Johnny's verbatim "portal to growth" is the anchor and must land unparaphrased. HARD RULE: only a true feedback story goes here; if a detail can't be told truly, tell less. The person who gave the feedback is honored, never villained — they opened the pane.*

## Section 4 — Vulnerable spaces: trusting the journey *(THE PORTAL)*
**Target: 6 min**

- Honor the cost honestly — this is why the portal stays shut: *"Let's not pretend. **It takes us into vulnerable spaces.** When you ask someone what they see, you hand them something sharp. The armor we wear around our blind pane wasn't stupid — it was protection. Somewhere along the way, feedback and criticism got tangled together, and we stopped being able to tell the mirror from the knife."*
- Untangle them: criticism wants to win; feedback wants you whole. *"You can feel the difference in your body. Criticism shrinks you. True feedback — even when it stings — makes you strangely larger."*
- Johnny's verbatim trust-note, delivered as companionship: *"**It requires us to trust the process of journeying forward.** You won't see where the portal leads before you walk through. That's not a design flaw. That's what makes it courage instead of a calculation."*
- Then the stakes, unflinching and verbatim: *"**The alternative is stagnation and rot.** Not staying safe — there is no staying safe. A self that stops receiving stops growing, and a thing that stops growing doesn't hold still. It rots. The pane you defend hardest becomes the room that molds."*
- *(Optional orchard callback — `[Johnny to confirm]`):* *"I live on a plum orchard, so I'll say it the way the trees taught me: a tree never sees its own dead branch. The orchardist does. And the tree that never gets that news doesn't stay the same tree — it starts dying from the branch in. Pruning isn't the opposite of growth. It's how the tree gets to keep growing."*

*Speaker note: This is the talk's most tender section — the room's resistance lives here, so it must feel accompanied, not lectured. "Stagnation and rot" is Johnny's phrase and stays verbatim and unhedged; its bluntness is the mercy. If using the orchard callback, it must be told as true (Johnny really lives on the orchard) and stays gentle — no one in the room is called rotten; the warning is about panes, not people.*

## Section 5 — The practice: asking bravely *(DEPTH & BREADTH, made Monday-usable)*
**Target: 8 min** · *(Audience Moment 2 lands here)*

- Frame the give: *"So how do we actually do this — walk to the pane and ask? Three small practices. None of them require a personality transplant. All of them require a half-inch of nerve."*
- **CHOOSE THE MIRROR:** not everyone earns the question. Choose someone who has seen you over time, wants you whole, and has nothing to win. *"The brave question in the wrong hands is just an invitation to be cut. In the right hands, it's the beginning of a bigger life. Choose someone who loves the person you're becoming, not just the person you've been."*
- **THE BRAVE QUESTION:** ask it plainly and small: *"'What's one thing you see in me that I might not see in myself?' One thing — not an inventory. You're opening a pane, not scheduling a demolition."* `[Johnny to fill: the brave question as YOU actually ask it — your real wording, if you have one you use or teach.]`
- **RECEIVE WITHOUT ARMOR:** the hardest practice in the talk: *"When the answer comes — and your chest tightens, and the courtroom in your head calls its first witness — you say the two hardest words in the English language: 'Thank you.' Not 'yes, but.' Not the context they're missing. Thank you. You can decide later what to build with it. Feedback is information, not instruction — you remain the author of your own becoming. But in the moment of receiving, your only job is to let the pane open."*
- **→ Audience Moment 2 (private write):** *"Two blanks, just for you: The person I would trust with the brave question is ______. And the thing I'm most afraid they'd say is ______."* 60–90 seconds. Then, gently: *"Notice — you already know both answers. The pane isn't as blind as it pretends. Somewhere in you, you already feel what's behind it. That feeling is the portal, glowing."*
- Close the section warm: *"One mirror. One question. Two words. That's the whole practice. Courage at walking pace."*

*Speaker note: This is "the give" — three moves, no acronym, no framework branding. "Feedback is information, not instruction" protects the room from feedback-as-tyranny — Johnny stays the author of his becoming and so do they; growth through the portal is chosen, never imposed. Every illustration universal or from Johnny's own life; never invented client stories. Keep Moment 2 fully private in every venue — it names real people.*

## Section 6 — Depth and breadth: the self uncovered *(DEPTH & BREADTH completes)*
**Target: 4 min**

- The exhale. Return to the window — and to the third pane held back in Section 3: *"Remember the pane nobody sees through yet — the unknown self? Here's the quiet secret of the window: every time the blind pane opens, that unknown pane shrinks too. What others hand you doesn't just correct you. It UNCOVERS you. Capacities you couldn't see. Rooms you didn't know your house had."*
- Johnny's verbatim payoff, delivered slowly, as the destination: *"**In this way, courage leads us forward to our own health — it allows us to uncover our unknown self and build upon our character, adding both depth and breadth.**"*
- Paint the two directions honestly and modestly: depth — more truly yourself than before, roots further down; breadth — bigger than you were, rooms open that were sealed. *"Not a different self. A larger one. Health, not heroics."*
- Set a true expectation: the window never finishes. Panes cloud over again; new blind spots grow as you grow. *"You don't graduate from the window. You just stop being afraid of it — and start treating every opened pane as what it always was: the portal, doing its work."* *(No promised outcomes.)*
- Callback to the cold open: *"And notice — no leap anywhere in sight. Just a question, a listening, and a self that's been quietly growing in both directions. That was the courage the whole time."*

*Speaker note: This is the destination — keep it spacious and modest; "depth and breadth" is Johnny's verbatim payoff and closes the loop. No transformation-industry language ("best self," "10x," "unstoppable"). The window imagery completes: blind pane open, unknown pane shrinking — say it once and let the hands show the rest.*
