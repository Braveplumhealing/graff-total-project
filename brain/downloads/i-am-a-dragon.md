## The poem, verbatim

I am a Dragon

I am a dragon humble and kind.
Those stories you hear, those stories aren't mine.
They are told by a man and his fear gets it wrong.

Let me tell you my story with my words and my song.
I was sent to your home from a land far away,
sent as a witness to your hunger and slave.

Your quarrels confuse with no why and no end
and your wants are so empty as you greedily spend.
You think you are different than your neighbor and kin
but I am a dragon and your differences - slim!
Your land is abundant - not fire and rock –
and yet you pretend that your wealth is for naught.
What if you changed and you see what I see
so come with me, be with me, trust me you'll see.
Your hearts are for love not your fear nor your hate.
And your minds are creative not for judgement nor crate.
Your hands carry comfort not this violence and blame
while your feet are to wander not to kick or for gain.
And your mouth it's for speaking kind words and refrain
and your eyes - they're the windows to the beauty each day.
Your love knows no limit believe it's true
and me as your witness
I tell you you're due.
Let go of your past - quit slurping your brew.
Forgive your old ways and begin from anew.
For I am your dragon, and I am honored by you.
With Love, D.

## Notes (Mr Rogers' read)

- **The menagerie completes.** Johnny's theology keeps arriving through animals the world
  has mis-storied: the **horse** broken by a cowboy (wildness lost) · the **eagle** living
  small in a coop (Soulnapped) · the **tall poppy** snuffed for standing above the rest ·
  and now the **dragon** — feared and slandered by stories "told by a man and his fear."
  Same teaching every time: the labeled being is not the broken one; the labeling is.
- **The body inventory is the Gift download in verse:** hearts FOR love, minds FOR
  creating, hands FOR comfort, feet FOR wandering, mouth FOR kind words, eyes FOR beauty —
  every faculty granted for a purpose, meant to be spent, not shuttered. (Poignant and
  private: the maker's hands line, from a shop teacher who gave a finger to his craft —
  never say this publicly unless Johnny does.)
- **The dragon is a witness, not a judge** — "I tell you you're due" is rising mercy in a
  dragon's mouth; "forgive your old ways and begin from anew" is the homecoming.
- **Possible futures (Johnny's call, parked):** a page in/companion to the book · a spoken
  piece for retreats or the Grove · a journal post · simply a private treasure. No use
  without his word.
