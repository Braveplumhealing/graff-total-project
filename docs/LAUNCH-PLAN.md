## Risks & guardrails

- **The say/do gap is the biggest risk.** Workshops and retreats — the top rungs — are described but not bookable. Every day the site promises what it can't fulfill, trust quietly erodes. This is why the events system is Phase 2.
- **Sequence discipline.** It's tempting to build the events system first, but if we do it before lighting analytics and closing the payment dead-end, we're building blind onto a leaky funnel. Hold the order.
- **Deposits vs. no refund policy.** The moment we take a deposit, someone will ask what happens if they can't come. Your one-sentence deposit terms come *before* the events system takes real money. Only you move or refund funds.
- **Sacred-language guardrail.** The triad is your own and load-bearing. We use only your confirmed words; the eagle image and anything unconfirmed stay *[Johnny to define]* and we ask.
- **Pricing-copy hype.** Raised rates invite the temptation to justify them with outcome claims or fake scarcity — all banned. The de-risker is the free call, not a pitch.
- **Zero-data discipline.** No traffic data exists until GoatCounter is lit. Any number stays an illustrative goal you choose; the homepage stats stay unasserted.
- **Sole-operator fragility.** Bus-factor of one. The Outlook inbox is the recovery root for everything; an expired domain is the one failure no backup can fix. Account armor + a named keyholder close this.
- **No placeholder events.** The events system waits for your first real workshop date — a calendar of invented events would break the no-invented-content rule.
